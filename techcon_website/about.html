History

TechCon was founded in the late 1990s by a small group of university students and startup founders who wanted a place to share emerging ideas in software, hardware, and design. The first TechCon was held in a converted lecture hall and ran for a single day. Attendance was under 500, but the event's spirit — open talks, hack sessions, and hands-on demos — set the tone for everything that followed.

As the web economy accelerated in the 2000s, TechCon grew quickly. Organizers introduced a multi-track schedule (developer, design, and business) and brought on professional event staff. The conference moved to a larger convention center and expanded from a single day to three days. Key developments in this era included: Sponsor pavilions that allowed startups to demo next to established companies. Women in Tech scholarship programs introduced to broaden participation. Industry panels featuring emerging standards and open-source governance

Mission

The heart of TechCon has always been guided by a commitment to innovation, collaboration, and meaningful technological progress. Each year, the conference brings together creators, builders, and thinkers who share a common belief: technology should empower people and shape a better future.
